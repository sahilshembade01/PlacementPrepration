Practice Questions

1)Quantitative Aptitude Questions

  • 1. If the ratio of the ages of A and B is 5:3 and the sum of their ages is 40 years, what is the age of A?

    • A) 20
    • B) 25
    • C) 30
    • D) 35

    Answer: B) 25

  • 2. What is the value of x if 3x + 5 = 20?

    • A) 5
    • B) 4
    • C) 10
    • D) 3

    Answer: B) 5

  • 3. A car travels 120 km in 3 hours. What is its speed?

    • A) 35 km/h
    • B) 40 km/h
    • C) 45 km/h
    • D) 50 km/h

    Answer: B) 40 km/h

  • 4. The sum of two consecutive integers is 65. What are the integers?

    • A) 32, 33
    • B) 31, 34
    • C) 30, 35
    • D) 33, 32

    Answer: A) 32, 33

  • 5. The average of five consecutive numbers is 20. What is the largest number?

    • A) 21
    • B) 22
    • C) 23
    • D) 24

    Answer: C) 23

  • 6. If the price of a commodity increases by 25%, by how much percent should the consumption be reduced to maintain the same expenditure?

    • A) 20%
    • B) 25%
    • C) 30%
    • D) 35%

    Answer: A) 20%

  • 7. Find the next number in the sequence: 2, 6, 12, 20, ?

    • A) 24
    • B) 30
    • C) 36
    • D) 42

    Answer: B) 30

  • 8. A sum of money doubles in 5 years at simple interest. What is the rate of interest per annum?

    • A) 10%
    • B) 12%
    • C) 15%
    • D) 20%

    Answer: A) 10%

  • 9. A bag contains 3 red balls, 4 green balls, and 5 blue balls. What is the probability of picking a red ball?

    • A) 1/3
    • B) 3/12
    • C) 1/4
    • D) 1/2

    Answer: B) 3/12 or 1/4

  • 10. If a man can complete a work in 12 days, how much time will he take to complete it with the help of a friend who can finish the work in 18 days?

    • A) 6 days
    • B) 7 days
    • C) 8 days
    • D) 9 days

    Answer: B) 7 days

  • 11. Find the compound interest on $1000 at 5% per annum for 2 years.

    • A) $50
    • B) $102.5
    • C) $105
    • D) $110

    Answer: B) $102.5

  • 12. A bat and ball together cost $110. If the bat costs $100 more than the ball, what is the cost of the ball?

    • A) $10
    • B) $20
    • C) $30
    • D) $50

    Answer: A) $10

  • 13. The perimeter of a rectangle is 48 meters, and its length is 14 meters. What is the width?

    • A) 10 meters
    • B) 12 meters
    • C) 15 meters
    • D) 20 meters

    Answer: B) 12 meters

  • 14. If 5x + 3 = 18, what is the value of x?

    • A) 3
    • B) 2
    • C) 5
    • D) 7

    Answer: B) 3

  • 15. The length of a rectangle is 3 times its width. If the area of the rectangle is 192 sq. cm, what is the width?

    • A) 8 cm
    • B) 6 cm
    • C) 12 cm
    • D) 10 cm

    Answer: A) 8 cm

  • 16. In a class of 40 students, 25% are absent. How many students are present?

    • A) 30
    • B) 35
    • C) 38
    • D) 28

    Answer: A) 30

  • 17. A train travels at a speed of 72 km/h. How long will it take to cover a distance of 180 km?

    • A) 2 hours
    • B) 3 hours
    • C) 4 hours
    • D) 5 hours

    Answer: B) 3 hours

  • 18. A man invests $5000 at a rate of 10% per annum compounded annually. How much will the investment be worth after 2 years?

    • A) $6100
    • B) $5525
    • C) $5500
    • D) $6025

    Answer: A) $6100

  • 19. What is the next number in the sequence: 1, 4, 9, 16, 25, ?

    • A) 36
    • B) 40
    • C) 42
    • D) 49

    Answer: A) 36



    • 2)logical Reasoning

  • 1. If all roses are flowers, and some flowers fade quickly, can we conclude that some roses fade quickly?

    • A) Yes
    • B) No
    • C) Cannot be determined
    • D) None of the above

    Answer: C) Cannot be determined

  • 2. John is taller than Mary, and Mary is taller than Lily. Who is the tallest?

    • A) John
    • B) Mary
    • C) Lily
    • D) Cannot be determined

    Answer: A) John

  • 3. If today is Monday, what day will it be 100 days from now?

    • A) Tuesday
    • B) Wednesday
    • C) Thursday
    • D) Friday

    Answer: B) Wednesday

  • 4. If all men are mortal and Socrates is a man, what can we conclude about Socrates?

    • A) Socrates is immortal
    • B) Socrates is mortal
    • C) Socrates is not a man
    • D) Cannot be determined

    Answer: B) Socrates is mortal

  • 5. If no birds can swim and some fish can swim, which of the following is true?

    • A) Some birds can swim
    • B) Some fish are birds
    • C) No fish can swim
    • D) No birds are fish

    Answer: D) No birds are fish

  • 6. If a train is moving at 60 km/h and it takes 30 minutes to cover a certain distance, how long will it take to cover double that distance?

    • A) 30 minutes
    • B) 60 minutes
    • C) 90 minutes
    • D) 120 minutes

    Answer: B) 60 minutes

  • 7. What comes next in the sequence: 2, 5, 10, 17, ?

    • A) 18
    • B) 26
    • C) 30
    • D) 34

    Answer: B) 26

  • 8. If three friends are standing in a line, and the one on the left is taller than the one in the middle, who is the shortest?

    • A) The one on the left
    • B) The one in the middle
    • C) The one on the right
    • D) Cannot be determined

    Answer: C) The one on the right

  • 9. If some flowers are red and some red flowers are roses, can we say that some roses are flowers?

    • A) Yes
    • B) No
    • C) Cannot be determined
    • D) None of the above

    Answer: A) Yes

  • 10. A man is facing east. He turns 90 degrees to the left and then 180 degrees to the right. In which direction is he now facing?

    • A) North
    • B) South
    • C) East
    • D) West

    Answer: B) South

  • 11. In a group of 100 people, 60 like football, 50 like basketball, and 40 like both. How many like only football?

    • A) 10
    • B) 20
    • C) 30
    • D) 50

    Answer: B) 20

  • 12. If all squares are rectangles and all rectangles are polygons, can we say that all squares are polygons?

    • A) Yes
    • B) No
    • C) Cannot be determined
    • D) None of the above

    Answer: A) Yes

  • 13. If all cats are mammals, and some mammals are dogs, can we conclude that some cats are dogs?

    • A) Yes
    • B) No
    • C) Cannot be determined
    • D) None of the above

    Answer: B) No

  • 14. A number is divisible by 4 if its last two digits form a number divisible by 4. Which of the following numbers is divisible by 4?

    • A) 124
    • B) 132
    • C) 145
    • D) 150

    Answer: A) 124

  • 15. If two pencils cost $1.50, how much will 6 pencils cost?

    • A) $3
    • B) $4.50
    • C) $5
    • D) $7.50

    Answer: B) $4.50

  • 16. If two friends start walking towards each other from different places, and one walks 3 miles per hour while the other walks 2 miles per hour, how long will it take them to meet if the distance between them is 10 miles?

    • A) 1 hour
    • B) 2 hours
    • C) 3 hours
    • D) 4 hours

    Answer: B) 2 hours

  • 17. What is the next number in the sequence: 5, 10, 20, 40, ?

    • A) 50
    • B) 60
    • C) 80
    • D) 100

    Answer: C) 80

  • 18. If you rearrange the letters of "VILLAIN", what word can you form?

    • A) VILLAIN
    • B) VIAL
    • C) VALID
    • D) NAIL

    Answer: D) NAIL

  • 19. If a clock shows 3:15, what angle is formed by the hour and minute hands?

    • A) 0 degrees
    • B) 15 degrees
    • C) 30 degrees
    • D) 45 degrees

    Answer: D) 45 degrees

  • 20. Which of the following is the odd one out?

    • A) Dog
    • B) Cat
    • C) Bird
    • D) Airplane

    Answer: D) Airplane
